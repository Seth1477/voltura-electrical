Residential Electrical FAQ

Everything Edmonton homeowners ask before booking

Do I need a permit for residential electrical work in Edmonton?
Yes — in Edmonton, the Safety Codes Act requires permits for most residential electrical work beyond simple device swaps. Panel upgrades, new branch circuits, basement renovations, EV charger installation, pot light circuits, and new service entrances all require an electrical permit and a City of Edmonton inspection. Voltura Electrical pulls all permits and schedules the inspection on your behalf — you do nothing. Working without a permit is illegal, voids home insurance, and creates problems when you sell.
How much does it cost to hire a residential electrician in Edmonton?
Edmonton electrician pricing varies by job type. Typical ranges: pot light installation $80–$150 per light (depending on attic access and drywall); Level 2 EV charger installation $600–$1,200 (panel location and conduit run affect cost); 200A panel upgrade starting at $3,500 all-inclusive; basement rough-in wiring $2,000–$5,000 depending on square footage. All Voltura quotes are itemized and provided in writing before work begins — no surprise invoices.

Yes. Voltura Electrical Services is a fully licensed electrical contractor in Alberta and carries comprehensive general liability insurance. Every electrician on our team holds a valid Alberta Journeyman Electrician certificate and works in compliance with the Alberta Electrical Code (CEC as adopted in Alberta). You receive a proper invoice, permit documentation, and inspection certificate for every permitted job — records that protect you as a homeowner.
Can you replace knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring in my older Edmonton home?
Yes — this is one of our most common residential services in Edmonton's older neighbourhoods. Homes built before 1980 frequently have knob-and-tube wiring or aluminum branch circuit wiring, both of which are fire hazards and commonly cause insurers to deny coverage or charge high premiums. Voltura provides full rewires, panel replacements, and targeted circuit upgrades to bring your home up to current CSA and Alberta Electrical Code standards. We assess your existing wiring and give you a clear, honest quote.
